10 ChatGPT Prompts You Should Know in 2025

Master these advanced ChatGPT prompts to get better results faster. From coding to writing, these techniques will transform how you use AI.

Tips & Tricks | 5 min read | 2025-02-12

ChatGPT is incredibly powerful, but knowing the right prompts makes all the difference. Here are 10 techniques that will dramatically improve your results.

1. The "Chain of Thought" Prompt

Instead of asking for an answer, ask ChatGPT to think step by step:

"Think step by step and then give me your answer about [topic]"

This forces deeper reasoning and often produces more accurate results.

2. Specify the Format You Want

Don't just ask for informationâ€”tell ChatGPT exactly how to present it:

"List these 10 tips in a table with columns: Tip, Example, and Why It Works"

3. Use System Prompts for Context

Set the stage before asking your actual question:

"You are an expert marketing consultant with 20 years of experience. Now help me improve my email open rates."

4. The "Contrast" Technique

Get better analysis by asking for multiple perspectives:

"What are the arguments for and against [decision]? Give me both sides equally."

5. Ask for Constraints

Ironically, limits spark creativity:

"Write a 50-word product description using only words starting with letters A through D."

6. The "Few-Shot" Approach

Give examples of what you want:

"Here are two examples of good headlines: [example 1], [example 2]. Now write 5 similar headlines for my blog post about [topic]."

7. Ask for Iterations

Get multiple versions to choose from:

"Give me 3 different versions of this email: [email]. Version 1 should be friendly, Version 2 professional, Version 3 urgent."

8. Use "What Would X Do?" Framing

Channel experts for better advice:

"What would Seth Godin do if he were launching a new product in a saturated market?"

9. Ask for the "Worst Case" First

Get the risks out of the way:

"What is the worst possible outcome of [decision], and how could we mitigate it?"

10. End with "Anything I Missed?"

One final prompt often unlocks the best insights:

"What important points about [topic] have I not considered yet?"

Bonus: The Master Prompt

Combine techniques for maximum effect:

"You are an expert [field]. Think step by step about [problem]. Give me 3 solutions ranked by effectiveness, with pros and cons for each. Also tell me what I might be missing."
