10 ChatGPT Prompts You Should Know in 2025

Master these advanced ChatGPT prompts to get better results faster. From coding to writing, these techniques will transform how you use AI.

Tips & Tricks | 8 min read | 2025-02-12

ChatGPT has transformed how we work, learn, and create. Yet most people still type simple queries and wonder why they do not get great results. The secret lies not in the AI itself, but in how you ask.

After testing thousands of prompts across writing, coding, analysis, and creative work, I have identified ten techniques that consistently deliver superior results. These are not tricks or hacks—they are proven communication strategies that work with how AI processes information.

1. The Chain of Thought Prompt

One of the most effective techniques is asking ChatGPT to think step by step before answering. This is called "Chain of Thought" prompting, and it dramatically improves accuracy on complex tasks.

The Prompt:

"Think step by step about [your question or problem]. Explain your reasoning process, then provide your final answer."

Why It Works: When ChatGPT breaks down its reasoning, it catches errors along the way. A study by Google researchers found that Chain of Thought prompting improved accuracy on math problems by over 50%.

Example Use Cases:

  • Complex math or logic problems
  • Multi-step business calculations
  • Comparing multiple options with pros and cons
  • Debugging code with multiple potential issues

2. Specify Your Output Format

Do not leave format to chance. Tell ChatGPT exactly how you want the information structured.

The Prompt:

"Provide this information as a [format: table/Markdown/list/JSON]. Include the following columns/fields: [list your requirements]."

Example:

"Create a comparison table comparing these 5 AI tools. Columns should be: Tool Name, Best For, Key Feature, Pricing Tier, and Overall Rating (1-10)."

3. The System Prompt Technique

Before asking your main question, set the context by defining who ChatGPT should be.

The Prompt:

"You are a [specific role: expert financial advisor/senior software engineer/award-winning copywriter] with [X years] of experience. Your specialty is [specific area]. Now, please [your actual question or task]."

Real Example:

"You are a senior software engineer with 15 years of experience in Python and machine learning. You have worked at Google and OpenAI. Your specialty is optimizing ML pipelines for production. Now, review this code snippet for performance issues and suggest improvements."

4. The Contrast and Compare Approach

Get better analysis by explicitly asking for multiple perspectives.

The Prompt:

"Analyze [topic] from three different perspectives: [perspective 1], [perspective 2], and [perspective 3]. For each perspective, explain the key arguments and evidence."

Example:

"Analyze the impact of remote work on productivity from three perspectives: employer, employee, and economic policy maker. For each, identify the main benefits and challenges."

5. The Constraint Challenge

Paradoxically, adding constraints sparks more creativity and often produces better results.

The Prompt:

"Create [content type] about [topic] with these constraints: [constraint 1], [constraint 2], [constraint 3]."

Creative Examples:

  • "Write a 50-word product description using only words with 3 or fewer syllables"
  • "Create a haiku that explains quantum computing"
  • "Write a tweet (280 chars max) that explains blockchain to a 10-year-old"
  • "Draft an email using only active voice sentences"

6. The Few-Shot Learning Prompt

Show ChatGPT examples of what you want, then ask for more of the same.

The Prompt:

"Here are 2-3 examples of [content type] I like:

[Example 1]
[Example 2]

Now create a new one about [your topic] following the same style and structure."

Use Case: Training ChatGPT to match your brand voice or writing style.

7. The Iteration Request

Never settle for the first draft. Ask for variations to choose from.

The Prompt:

"Create 3 different versions of [content]:

Version 1: [tone/style: professional and formal]
Version 2: [tone/style: casual and friendly]
Version 3: [tone/style: urgent and action-oriented]

All versions should cover the same key points."

8. Channel an Expert

Ask ChatGPT to channel specific experts for unique perspectives.

The Prompt:

"What would [famous person or expert] say about [topic]? Base your response on their known views, writings, and philosophies."

Examples:

  • "What would Warren Buffett say about this investment opportunity?"
  • "How would Steve Jobs approach product marketing for this new app?"
  • "What advice would Maya Angelou give for overcoming writer's block?"

9. The Risk Assessment Framework

Get comprehensive risk analysis by explicitly asking for it.

The Prompt:

"Analyze the risks of [decision/plan] across these categories: financial, operational, reputational, and regulatory. For each risk, rate the likelihood (high/medium/low) and impact (high/medium/low), then suggest mitigation strategies."

10. The Comprehensive Review Prompt

End every request with a powerful final question.

The Prompt:

"I have covered the main points about [topic]. What important perspectives, counterarguments, or related topics have I not considered that I should research further?"

Bonus: The Master Prompt Template

Combine multiple techniques for maximum impact:

"You are a [role]. Think step by step about [topic]. Provide your analysis in [format] covering: [key points]. Also tell me what I might be missing."

Final Thoughts

Great prompting is a skill that improves with practice. Start with one technique this week. Notice how responses change. Soon, these strategies will become second nature.

The difference between average and exceptional AI output is not the AI—it is the human providing direction. Master these techniques, and you will consistently get results that amaze.
