Midjourney Prompt Engineering Secrets Nobody Tells You

Stop getting generic AI art. These advanced prompt techniques will make your Midjourney images stand out from 99% of users.

AI Art | 6 min read | 2025-02-12

Most Midjourney users type "beautiful landscape" and wonder why their images look generic. Here is what the pros know that you do not.

1. Weights Matter More Than Words

Use double colons to weight important elements:

"cat::2 dog::1 garden::1.5"

This tells Midjourney to emphasize the cat twice as much as the dog.

2. Negative Weights Remove Elements

Get rid of unwanted objects:

"portrait --no glasses, watermarks, text"

The --no parameter tells Midjourney what to exclude.

3. Aspect Ratios Change Everything

Different ratios suit different purposes:

"--ar 16:9" (widescreen, landscapes)

"--ar 9:16" (vertical, social media)

"--ar 1:1" (square, Instagram)

"--ar 2:3" (portrait photography)

4. Reference Images Work Better Than Descriptions

Upload a reference image and ask for variations:

"[upload image] Similar lighting and mood, but different subject in the same style"

5. Lighting Terms Are Magic Words

Great lighting makes mediocre prompts shine:

  • "cinematic lighting" - movie-quality depth
  • "golden hour" - warm sunset light
  • "volumetric lighting" - rays and atmosphere
  • "rim lighting" - dramatic edge light
  • "softbox lighting" - even, studio quality

6. Camera Names Work Like Magic

Reference real cameras for consistent quality:

"shot on Hasselblad, 85mm f/1.4"

"35mm film grain, Leica M6"

7. Artist Styles Override Everything

Get any art style instantly:

"in the style of Studio Ghibli"

"oil painting by John Singer Sargent"

"art deco poster, 1920s style"

8. Chaos and Seed Control Variation

Get more creative options:

"--chaos 50" (more unusual results)

"--seed 12345" (reproducible results)

9. The Version Matters

Different versions excel at different things:

  • V6 - Best text accuracy, most realistic
  • V5.2 - Great balance of creativity and control
  • Niji - Anime and illustration style

10. Quality Over Quantity

Fewer, well-crafted prompts beat spamming:

"A lone samurai at dusk, Mount Fuji silhouette, cherry blossoms falling, cinematic lighting, shot on 35mm, film grain, --ar 16:9 --v 6"

Bonus: The Pro Formula

[subject] [setting] [lighting] [camera/lens] [style/artist] --ar [ratio] --v 6
