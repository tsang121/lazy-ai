Midjourney Prompt Engineering Secrets Nobody Tells You

Stop getting generic AI art. These advanced prompt techniques will make your Midjourney images stand out from 99% of users.

AI Art | 9 min read | 2025-02-12

Most Midjourney users type "beautiful landscape" and wonder why their images look like everyone else's. The truth is, Midjourney rewards those who understand its language.

After generating over 10,000 images and studying what separates amateur results from professional work, I have compiled the techniques that the pros use but rarely discuss.

Understanding Weight

Midjourney uses double colons (::) to assign importance to different elements. This is the most powerful yet underused feature.

Basic Syntax:

subject::weight

Examples:

cat::2 dog::1 garden::1.5

What This Means: The cat is twice as important as the dog, and the garden is 1.5 times as important. Midjourney will allocate visual attention accordingly.

Negative Weights Remove Elements

The --no parameter tells Midjourney what to exclude. This is how you eliminate unwanted objects.

The Command:

your prompt here --no element1, element2, element3

Examples:

professional headshot --no glasses, watermarks, text, logo

romantic dinner --no children, pets, modern elements

vintage car --no rust, damage, modern buildings

Pro Tip: Use negative weights for style elements too:

mysterious forest --no cartoon, anime, bright colors

Aspect Ratios Matter More Than You Think

Different ratios fundamentally change how Midjourney composes images.

Common Ratios:

  • --ar 16:9 - Cinematic widescreen, landscapes, storytelling
  • --ar 9:16 - Vertical, social media, portraits
  • --ar 1:1 - Square, Instagram, product shots
  • --ar 4:5 - Portrait photography standard
  • --ar 2:3 - Classic portrait, posters
  • --ar 3:2 - Classic landscape, photography
  • --ar 21:9 - Ultra-widescreen, panoramic

Reference Images Are Magic

Upload a reference image and Midjourney can match its style, lighting, or composition.

How to Use:

  • Upload image to Discord (drag and drop)
  • Right-click and "Copy Link"
  • Use the link in your prompt

The Prompt:

[image link] Similar lighting and mood, different subject in the same style

Style Reference:

[image link] --iw 2

The --iw Parameter: Image weight. Higher values (0.5-2) make Midjourney follow the reference more closely.

Lighting Is Everything

Great lighting makes mediocre prompts shine. These terms are magic words.

Cinematic Lighting Terms:

  • "cinematic lighting" - Movie-quality depth and drama
  • "golden hour" - Warm sunset light
  • "blue hour" - Cool twilight light
  • "volumetric lighting" - Rays and atmosphere
  • "rim lighting" - Dramatic edge light
  • "softbox lighting" - Even, studio quality
  • "chiaroscuro" - Strong light/dark contrast
  • "rembrandt lighting" - Classic portrait lighting

Camera Terminology Transforms Results

Midjourney understands photography terminology. Use it to get consistent, professional results.

Camera Models:

shot on Hasselblad 500C, 85mm f/1.4

35mm film grain, Leica M6

shot on Canon EOS R5, 50mm

Lens Effects:

  • "85mm" - Portrait compression, background blur
  • "35mm" - Wide-angle environmental portraits
  • "50mm" - Natural perspective
  • "f/1.4" - Shallow depth of field, bokeh
  • "f/8" - Sharp throughout, product photography

Artist Styles Override Everything

Reference specific artists for instant art direction.

Classical Artists:

in the style of John Singer Sargent

oil painting by Vermeer

watercolor style of Turner

Modern Artists:

in the style of Studio Ghibli

art by Greg Rutkowski

style of Alphonse Mucha

Chaos and Seed Control

Control variation and reproducibility.

Chaos Parameter (--chaos or --c):

prompt --c 50

Values: 0-100. Higher values produce more unusual, creative results.

Seed Parameter:

prompt --seed 12345

Version Selection Changes Everything

Different versions excel at different tasks.

Version 6 (V6):

  • Best text accuracy
  • Most realistic photography
  • Best for detailed character work
  • Requires more descriptive prompts

Niji Models:

  • Niji 6 - Anime and illustration style
  • Best for manga, anime art, cartoons

Quality Settings

The --q parameter controls processing time and detail.

  • --q 0.25 - Faster, less detail
  • --q 0.5 - Default, good balance
  • --q 1 - Higher detail, slower

The Pro Formula

Combine everything into a master template:

[subject] [setting/location] [lighting] [camera/lens] [style/artist] --ar [ratio] --v 6

Example:

a lone samurai at dusk, Mount Fuji silhouette, cherry blossoms falling, cinematic lighting, shot on 35mm film, 85mm f/1.4, in the style of old Japanese prints --ar 16:9 --v 6

Final Tips for Professional Results

  1. Be Specific: "portrait of a woman" → "portrait of a 35-year-old Scandinavian woman with blue eyes, natural light from window, candid expression"
  2. Use Collective Nouns: "a crowd" → "a bustling crowd of market vendors"
  3. Mention Materials: "jewelry made of obsidian and silver"
  4. Describe Textures: "weathered leather", "polished marble", "rough concrete"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Too Short Prompts: V6 rewards description. Be specific.
  • Ignoring Negative Space: Mention what should NOT be in the background.
  • Forgetting Aspect Ratio: --ar 1:1 is default, not always best.
  • Mixing Incompatible Styles: Realistic + Cartoon rarely works well.

Final Thoughts

Midjourney is a language as much as it is a tool. These techniques are the vocabulary and grammar of that language. Master them, and you can create virtually any image you can imagine.
